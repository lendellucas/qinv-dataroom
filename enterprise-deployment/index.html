QINV enterprise architecture

Data security without losing functionality

A reference architecture for delivering the QINV platform for deployment inside an enterprise-controlled environment, with QINV holding only temporary, approved access for updates, maintenance, and support. Sensitive data remains inside the institution's perimeter.

Central thesis: for a large institution, QINV should not be treated as one more external SaaS receiving PII and financial data. The enterprise model separates software, data, and intelligence: QINV delivers the platform to run inside the institution's environment; the data plane stays under the institution's control; intelligence features are consumed through APIs without sending identifiable client data outside the approved perimeter.

Executive summaryCustody-minimizing

1. The problem is not functionality. It is custody, data egress, and operational exposure.

A large institution's concern is correct: identifiable client data, portfolios, suitability, communications, and documents create operational, regulatory, and reputational risk. The answer should not be "trust QINV." The answer should be: the platform can be installed inside the institution's environment, with no need for sensitive data to leave that environment or be operationally custodied by QINV.

1. The institution controls the data plane

PII, portfolios, suitability, communications, logs, and keys all remain inside the institution's environment, governance, and policies.

2. QINV delivers the software

Product, UX, workflows, templates, releases, and maintenance are decoupled from custody of sensitive data. QINV accesses the environment only on a temporary, approved, and auditable basis.

3. Functionality preserved

Portfolio X-ray, reports, CRM, messaging, tickets, operations, AI, and governance all remain available. Intelligence is consumed through approved APIs and gateways, with no need to send identifiable client data to QINV.

Design principle: deliver the platform to operate inside the institution's perimeter and reduce the operational trust required in QINV.

What changes

The architecture keeps sensitive risk inside the institution's perimeter: identity, keys, logs, financial data, communications, policies, and integrations.

What does not change

Product experience, operational journey, reports, intelligence, and workflows all remain available through private deployment and controlled APIs.

The choice should reflect the institution's risk appetite and the level of control a systemically important organization requires.

If the case evolves toward distribution at scale, migrate to an embedded / white-label model inside the institution's ecosystem.

Security controls

5. Enterprise controls that turn risk into governance

The design must make explicit who controls identity, data, keys, logs, access, AI, and integrations.

PII Vault and pseudonymization

Real identity remains in the institution's vault. The application operates with canonical IDs, household IDs, portfolio IDs, and the attributes required for the workflow.

BYOK / HYOK

Encryption with keys controlled by the institution via KMS/HSM. QINV does not control the master key for sensitive data.

RBAC + ABAC

Permissions by role, portfolio, office, channel, data, action, context, and criticality. QINV support operates without PII by default and with temporary access when necessary.

Immutable audit trail

A record of access, generation, recommendation, approval, send, integration, failure, applied policy, and AI/prompt version.

Institution-approved AI Gateway

AI only through an institution-approved gateway/API, with redaction and minimization before sending. No identifiable client information to external models or providers.

Read-only first

A pilot can start as an intelligence and productivity layer, with no automatic financial execution or change to records.

AI governance

6. AI with data minimization, validation, and human approval

The architecture allows intelligence features to be consumed through institution-approved APIs or gateways, with no need to send identifiable client information. Prompts use minimized, redacted, or pseudonymized data, with validation and human approval.

1. Data minimization
2. PII redaction
3. Approved model
4. Validation layer
5. Human approval

1. Data minimization

Send to AI only the necessary, minimized, or pseudonymized attributes: profile, composition, alerts, and objective.

2. PII redaction

Remove names, tax IDs, phone, email, documents, and direct identifiers.

3. Approved model

Use an internal model or a gateway approved by the institution.

4. Validation layer

Validate schema, language, suitability, disclaimers, evidence, and policies.

5. Human approval

An advisor / compliance approves recommendations, reports, and sensitive communications.

Allowed payload example

Profile: moderate; allocation: fixed income 72%, equities 18%, alternatives 10%; alerts: issuer concentration, low liquidity; objective: generate a portfolio X-ray explanation for advisor review.

Data that does not need to go to AI

Name, tax ID, phone, email, address, account, documents, full messages, direct identifiers, and unnecessary personal history.

Message to the institution: QINV does not need to receive raw client data or take it outside the institution's environment. Intelligence features can be accessed via API, with minimized, redacted, or pseudonymized payloads and governance approved by the institution.

Closing message

10. The next conversation should be about controlled architecture, not generic trust

The conversation with a large institution should move from "security in the abstract" into the design of perimeter, responsibilities, and residual risk.

Materials to prepare

  • Summarized threat model
  • QINV vs. institution responsibility matrix
  • AI governance model
  • Access and support policy
  • Pilot SOW with read-only scope

Questions for the institution

  • Approved cloud/VPC and standards
  • IAM/SSO, groups, and authorization
  • Permitted AI providers/gateways
  • Communications retention and archiving
  • SIEM/logging and audit requirements
  • Minimum data for the first pilot

Final message: the QINV platform can be positioned as an enterprise-grade layer of intelligence and wealth operation, delivered for deployment inside the institution's control perimeter, not as an external SaaS concentrating sensitive data.
