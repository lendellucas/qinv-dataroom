1. System overview

QINV’s system is a multi-layer investment research infrastructure. It combines a proprietary investment algorithm with a formal mathematical foundation, eight years of iterative improvement, systematic research workflows, macro regime analysis, asset-level evaluation, portfolio optimization, trading strategy research, AI-assisted engineering, classical ML, and controlled validation cycles.

Research layers

Macro regime, asset evaluation, portfolio allocation, tactical/trading opportunity research, and validation.

Execution model

AI agents and human governance jointly support hypothesis generation, implementation, testing, review, and documentation on a continuous basis.

Validation model

Candidate ideas are evaluated through repeatable backtests, Walk Forward Optimization, robustness checks, and evidence artifacts.

4. Architecture

The architecture is organized around a research-to-validation loop. Macro and asset-level research provide candidate views. Portfolio and trading engines convert candidate views into allocations or strategy candidates. Validation systems test candidate behavior. Results and diagnostics return to the next research cycle through self-recursive learning.

7. Portfolio allocation and solver engine

The allocation layer converts macro and asset-level views into exact asset percentages under constraints. This layer is separate from macro classification and from trading signal research.

Solver breadth

25+ solver / objective types exist in the algorithm engine dispatch, including risk/return, diversification, regret, CVaR, robust, direct allocation, and stochastic-dominance style research.

Constraint system

Allocation is shaped by bounds, benchmark behavior, portfolio weight rules, stochastic-dominance constraints, short/passive structure where applicable, and risk controls.

Backtest integration

Allocation outputs are parsed, simulated, validated, and connected to backtest returns and performance statistics.

9. Walk Forward Optimization and candidate evaluation

Walk Forward Optimization represents repeated candidate evaluation across folds. It is used to evaluate strategy configurations under changing data windows and reduce dependence on a single historical fit. The process is general across supported assets and strategy families.

1,000

Walk Forward Optimization trials per fold in the current WFO configuration pattern.

≈5,000

Candidate evaluations per full WFO retrain cycle, assuming approximately five active/scheduled folds and 1,000 trials per fold.

Candidate evaluation

A trial is a candidate parameter-set / strategy-configuration evaluation within a strategy family.

12. Where QINV’s architecture differs

This section explains the technical moat in NDA-safe language. The claim is not that QINV has merely added AI to an investment process; the advantage is the combination of proprietary algorithmic history, integrated quant infrastructure, validation discipline, and agent-accelerated research loops operating together as one system.

Accumulated algorithmic IP

The core investment logic has a formal mathematical origin and roughly eight years of refinement. Competitors can buy tools, hire researchers, or access LLMs, but they cannot instantly recreate the accumulated decisions, failures, validations, and domain-specific improvements embedded in the system.

Full-stack quant engine

QINV is not a single model. It is a coordinated stack spanning macro regime analysis, asset radar, allocation, trading research, solver selection, backtesting, WFO, robustness checks, and agentic research workflows.

AI-native operating model

AI agents increase research and engineering throughput across hypothesis generation, implementation, testing, review, and documentation while human governance controls promotion and deployment decisions.

Why this is difficult to copy

  • Compounded research history: years of iteration encode many negative results and refinements that are invisible from the outside.
  • Integrated architecture: macro, asset, allocation, trading, and validation layers are connected rather than operated as disconnected tools.
  • Solver breadth: the allocation layer supports many objective/constraint families instead of relying on one static portfolio construction method.
  • Validation habit: candidates are filtered through repeatable evidence, not promoted from single backtests or qualitative conviction.
  • Agentic velocity: parallel AI-assisted workflows compress research-cycle time without removing human risk control.

Why the architecture is robust

  • Mathematical depth: the system is grounded in formal optimization, statistical learning, and portfolio-construction logic.
  • Production-oriented design: research artifacts are connected to tests, configurations, validations, and reproducible outputs.
  • Multi-horizon capability: the same platform supports medium/long-term allocation research and tactical in/out strategy research.
  • Closed feedback loop: results, drawdowns, diagnostics, and review findings feed the next research cycle.
  • Governed AI adoption: AI is used as an accelerator around the research system, not as an unvalidated decision substitute.
